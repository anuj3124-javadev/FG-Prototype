Tomato Early Blight 92% confidence

Description

Early blight is a common fungal disease that affects tomato plants. It appears as concentric dark spots on leaves that eventually yellow and die.

Symptoms
  • Dark brown spots with concentric rings on leaves
  • Yellowing around the spots
  • Leaf drop starting from the bottom of the plant
  • Dark, sunken lesions on stems and fruits
Recommended Treatment
  • Remove and destroy infected leaves
  • Apply copper-based fungicide every 7-10 days
  • Use neem oil as an organic alternative
  • Improve air circulation around plants
  • Avoid overhead watering to keep foliage dry
Prevention Tips
  • Rotate crops annually to prevent disease buildup
  • Choose disease-resistant tomato varieties
  • Ensure proper spacing between plants for air circulation
  • Apply mulch to prevent soil splashing onto leaves
  • Water at the base of plants early in the day
